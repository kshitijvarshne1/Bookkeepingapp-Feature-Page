    What is Expense Tracking and How to Track it in Bookkeeping App?
    19 May, 2021
    What is Expense Tracking -
    Keeping a record of expenses is an important task for any small business. Expense tracking helps you to understand the financial health of your business, and improve your cash flow.
    Expense tracking is a method where a business records all types of expenses and spends.
    Benefits of Expenses Tracking -
    1. Become aware of total expenses - Tracking expenses helps to manage the better cash flow. Lack of cash is the main reason for most of the business failures. Tracking daily expenses helps you to understand where you are spending most, where you need to control the spending, where you can spend more, etc.
    2. Better control on profitability of business - Expense tracking helps to understand where are spending most, where you can spend less to reduce losses, where you can spend more to improve the profitability.
    3. Helps to achieve financial objectives - Expense tracking can be helpful to achieve your financial objectives. You can understand where you should spend most to achieve your business goals, where you need to spend today to achieve your yearly financial objective.
    How to Track Expenses in Bookkeeping App -
    1. You can record all expenses in purchase entry type
    2. To record inventory expenses - add product name, client name, price, quantity, cash amount. Total amount and Credit is auto-calculated.
         a. Total Amount = Price * Quantity
         b. Credit = Total Amount - Cash Amount
    3. You can create a separate book for every type of expenses
    4. Track all the expenses on the home page
         a. Track total purchase for all the expenses.
         b. Use Filter to track expenses by date
         c. Use Search bar to track expenses by product names or client names
         d. You can also track total expenses by cash & credit
    Benefits of Digital Expense Tracking
    1. Identify expenses saving opportunities
    2. Analyse all expenses
    3. Fast calculations
    4. Remove risk of errors
    5. Better transparency
    6. Free and secure data
